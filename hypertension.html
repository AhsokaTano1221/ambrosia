Hypertension

Explain why blood pressure can be dangerous even when symptoms are quiet, and why screening matters.

Identification Tips

Many people feel no symptoms, which is why routine checks are often more useful than waiting for discomfort.

Symptoms

Often silent, but may include headaches, dizziness, or vision changes.

Prevention

Blood pressure checks, movement, lower salt intake, stress care, and routine follow-up.

When to act

Seek evaluation if you have repeated high readings, severe headache, chest pain, or confusion.

What to know

Hypertension often overlaps with heart disease, stroke prevention, and kidney health.
